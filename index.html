The Problem

People don't need summaries.
They need understanding.

😔

Readers Give Up

Millions of motivated people start complex books every year — philosophy, economics, theology — and quit partway through. Not because they're not smart enough, but because they lack adaptive guidance.

📋

Summaries Don't Teach

Tools like Blinkist, ChatGPT, and NotebookLM summarize or answer questions — but they don't build real comprehension. Reading a summary is not the same as understanding the text.

🎯

One Size Fits None

Traditional study guides and generic AI chatbots can't adapt to your unique background, knowledge gaps, or learning style. Everyone gets the same explanation regardless of what they actually need.

"The unexamined life is not worth living." — Plato, Apology

🤖
Socrates isn't saying life has no value — he's saying that reflection is what gives life meaning. Think of it like this: imagine eating an incredible meal but never tasting it. That's what an unexamined life is like.
🤖
Why do you think Socrates says this at his trial, facing death? What does that tell us about his values?
Maybe because he'd rather die than stop questioning things?
👤
